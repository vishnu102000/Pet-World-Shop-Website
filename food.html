How To Choose The Right Dog Food?

To determine if a dog food is top-notch or not, follow the below tips;



Tailor the Food to Your Dog's Age and Health:

Just like humans, dogs have different nutritional requirements based on age and health condition. For example, a young and energetic pup will require more protein and calories to support its growth, while an older dog may need less protein and more fibre to support their digestive system. Therefore, it's important to tailor your dog's food to their specific needs.



Decode the Labels:

Reading the ingredients on the dog food label can be like deciphering hieroglyphics. However, it's essential to understand what you're feeding your dog. Make sure that the first ingredient listed is a high-quality source of protein, like chicken, beef, or fish. Stay away from foods that contain meat by-products, fillers and artificial preservatives. The clearer the label, the better the food!



Learn the Lingo:

Let's face it, some dog food packaging is confusing, but the terminology used can give you insight into the food you're buying. Knowing the difference between "grain-free" and "gluten-free" can help you choose the right food for your dog's dietary needs.

Pedigree

Having a pedigree means that the dog, along with their parents, grandparents, great-grandparents etc were Purebred dogs officially registered with the Kennel Club, and you can trace their lineage (and any show titles they might have won) going back several generations. As many breeds have inherited health issues, being able to trace lines back can help you become more aware of your dog’s health situation and give more clues to any issues they might have inherited.
